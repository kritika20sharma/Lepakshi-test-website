Artist's Field Journal · Pen & Ink Studies

Seventeen Studies
in Ink & Stone

Drawn by Dr A S Raja Rao across seventeen visits, in the tradition of measured devotion.

Chapter IEntry — The Outer World

The first sight of Lepakshi — the silhouette of the gopuram against open sky, the outer parikramas that circle the sacred ground.

Study I · 1The Outer Parikramas

A single ink traverse of both circumambulation circuits — note the shift in cornice height between the first and second ring. Hover to reveal the temple today.

Study I · 2Gopuram & Mandapa

The Dravidian tower in elevation — seven storeys of diminishing yali pilasters, capped by a barrel-vault shikhara unique to the Vijayanagara idiom

Chapter IIGuardians at the Threshold

Before you enter the inner world, the dvarapalas stand watch — fierce, adorned, eternal.

Study II · 1Veerabhadra

Eighteen arms bearing weapons of cosmic destruction — the gana lord born from Shiva's rage

Study II · 2Dvarapala & Ganesha

Abundance paired with the removal of obstacles at every entrance

Study II · 3The Warrior at the Gate

Tribhanga stance — weight over one hip. Signals potential force, not rest

Chapter IIIFigures of the Mandapa

The pillared halls hold a silent congregation — deities, apsaras, and celestial attendants carved into living stone, now alive again in ink.

Study III · 1Deity in the Niche

The cusped archway frames a tiered mukhamandapa — a Chalukyan compositional device refined at Vijayanagara

Study III · 2The Abhaya Mudra

Palm outward — the oldest gesture of protection in South Asian sculpture. Hover to see the hall it inhabits.

Study III · 3Celestial Attendant

High chignon, elongated fingers — a vidyadhara, knowledge-bearer of the celestial court

Study III · 4Four-Armed Deity

Conch, discus, mace, lotus — a Vaishnava form within a predominantly Shaiva temple complex

Study III · 5The Lion Plinth

The sardula base — half-horse, half-lion — appears on every major pillar shaft at Lepakshi

Study III · 6Apsara

Atibhanga — triple curve of the body — conveying sensuous celestial grace unique to Vijayanagara figuration

Study III · 7Apsara with Lotus

The half-open lotus — unfolding creation — a signature motif of Krishnadevaraya's patronage at Lepakshi

Study III · 8Amid Scrolling Foliage

The kirtimukha consumes its own body at the arch apex — horror vacui in stone

Chapter IVCosmic Forms — The Sanctum

In the innermost chamber, the divine takes its most expansive form — multi-armed, multi-headed, treading the earth. Here, time folds.

Study IV · 1Brahma — The Creator

Rarely depicted as primary deity — Lepakshi's Brahma implies a fifth head via the elevated lotus seat

Study IV · 2The Wrathful Form

The trampled figure — not enemy but ego — motif of inner conquest on all four sanctuary pillars

Study IV · 3Cosmic Dance

Ten arms in perfect radial symmetry — the geometry of creation, frozen in stone

Study IV · 4Veerabhadra Supreme

The presiding deity in full regalia — hover to reveal the painted fresco above the sanctum door

"What the chisel wrought in stone, the pen has written in devotion."

— Dr A S Raja Rao
