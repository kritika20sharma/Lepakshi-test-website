Le Pakshi —

Rise, O Bird

Origin · Mythology

"Where the sacred bird Jatayu fell wounded, where the divine breathed life back into the earth — here stands Lepakshi, born of a god's compassion."

According to Hindu mythology, Lepakshi is the hallowed ground where Jatayu — the divine eagle — collapsed after his valiant battle with Ravana to save Goddess Sita. Lord Rama, discovering the fallen bird, uttered the words "Le Pakshi" (Rise, O Bird), blessing this sacred land with its name and its legend.

Built in the 16th century under the Vijayanagara Empire by chieftains Virupanna and Viranna Nayaka, the temple complex at Lepakshi remains one of India's finest examples of Dravidian artistry — a monument where every carved surface is a prayer crystallised in time.

Monolithic Marvel

The Great Nandi

India's largest monolithic Nandi bull stands 4.5 metres tall and 8 metres long, carved from a single granite boulder. Adorned with delicate stone jewellery, this sacred guardian gazes with serene devotion toward the presiding deity.

Engineering Mystery

The Hanging Pillar

Among Lepakshi's 70 exquisitely carved pillars, one defies gravity — suspended from the ceiling without touching the ground. Visitors traditionally pass a cloth beneath it as a token of witnessing this unexplained miracle.

Learn More →
Living Murals

The Frescoes

The ceilings of Lepakshi bear some of India's finest Vijayanagara-era frescoes — vivid murals of the Mahabharata, Ramayana, and the legends of Shiva, painted in natural pigments that retain their brilliance five centuries on.

The Sacred Footprint

Sita Pada —
The Divine
Impression

Carved into the living rock of Lepakshi is a pair of giant footprints, believed by many to be the sacred imprints of Goddess Sita as she passed through this hallowed land during the events of the Ramayana.

Standing over a metre in length, the Sita Pada remains one of Lepakshi's most venerated relics. Pilgrims travel from across the subcontinent to pay homage, leaving offerings of flowers and prayer at the base of these ancient impressions.
